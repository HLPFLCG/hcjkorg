## Part 1: Pinterest Business Account Setup

### Why Pinterest for Photography?
Pinterest is a visual discovery platform perfect for photographers. Users actively search for photography inspiration, making it ideal for:
- Showcasing your portfolio
- Driving traffic to your website
- Reaching clients planning weddings, events, and portrait sessions
- Building brand awareness

### Step-by-Step Pinterest Setup

#### 1. Create Pinterest Business Account
1. Go to [pinterest.com/business/create](https://www.pinterest.com/business/create)
2. Click "Create account"
3. Choose "Create a business account"
4. Fill in your information:
   - **Business Name:** HCJK Collection
   - **Website:** https://www.hcjk.org
   - **Country:** Your location
   - **Language:** English
   - **Business Type:** Professional Services
   - **Business Description:** "Professional photography services specializing in weddings, portraits, and artistic photography. Capturing life's precious moments with elegance and creativity."

#### 2. Claim Your Website
1. Go to Settings ‚Üí Claimed accounts
2. Click "Claim" next to Website
3. Add HTML tag to your website's `<head>` section:
   ```html
   <meta name="p:domain_verify" content="[your-verification-code]"/>
   ```
4. Verify the claim

#### 3. Optimize Your Profile
- **Profile Photo:** Use your HCJK Collection logo (HH-4.png)
- **Cover Image:** Create a 800x450px banner showcasing your best work
- **Username:** @hcjkcollection (if available)
- **About Section:**
  ```

  üåü Based in [Your Location]
  üìß Book your session: info@hcjk.org

  ```

#### 4. Create Boards (Recommended Structure)
1. **Wedding Photography** - Your wedding portfolio
2. **Portrait Photography** - Individual and family portraits
3. **Engagement Sessions** - Couple photography
4. **Landscape Photography** - Nature and scenic shots
5. **Urban Photography** - Cityscape and street photography
6. **Behind the Scenes** - Your process and equipment
7. **Photography Tips** - Educational content
8. **Client Testimonials** - Reviews and feedback
9. **Seasonal Sessions** - Holiday and seasonal photography
10. **Inspiration** - Mood boards and style guides

#### 5. Pin Strategy
- **Pin Frequency:** 5-10 pins per day
- **Best Times:** 8-11 PM (when users plan events)
- **Image Specs:** 1000x1500px (2:3 ratio)
- **Pin Descriptions:** 
  - Include keywords: "wedding photography," "portrait photographer," "[your city] photographer"
  - Add call-to-action: "Book your session at hcjk.org"
  - Use 3-5 relevant hashtags

#### 6. Content Ideas
- Individual photos from sessions (with permission)
- Before/after editing comparisons
- Photography tips and tutorials
- Client testimonials with photos
- Seasonal photography guides
- Location spotlights
- Equipment recommendations
- Pricing package graphics
- Blog post graphics

#### 7. Pinterest SEO Optimization
- Use keywords in:
  - Board titles and descriptions
  - Pin titles and descriptions
  - Profile description
- Common photography keywords:
  - "wedding photographer [city]"
  - "engagement photos"
  - "family portraits"
  - "professional photography"
  - "photography packages"

#### 8. Enable Rich Pins
1. Add Open Graph meta tags to your website (already done!)
2. Validate at [developers.pinterest.com/tools/url-debugger](https://developers.pinterest.com/tools/url-debugger/)
3. Apply for Rich Pins

#### 9. Pinterest Analytics
- Track which pins drive the most traffic
- Monitor engagement rates
- Identify top-performing boards
- Adjust strategy based on data

## Part 2: Facebook Business Page Setup

### Why Facebook for Photography?
Facebook remains one of the most effective platforms for local businesses:
- Large user base actively planning events
- Powerful advertising tools
- Review and recommendation features
- Event promotion capabilities
- Direct messaging with potential clients

### Step-by-Step Facebook Setup

#### 1. Create Facebook Business Page
1. Go to [facebook.com/pages/create](https://www.facebook.com/pages/create)
2. Click "Get Started"
3. Choose "Business or Brand"
4. Fill in page information:
   - **Page Name:** HCJK Collection
   - **Category:** Photographer
   - **Additional Categories:** Wedding Photography, Portrait Photographer
   - **Bio:** "Professional photography services capturing life's precious moments with elegance and artistry. Specializing in weddings, portraits, and events."

#### 2. Complete Page Setup
- **Profile Picture:** HCJK Collection logo (HH-4.png) - 170x170px

- **Email:** info@hcjk.org

- **Location:** Your business address (if you have a studio)

## Part 3: Cross-Platform Integration

### Link Your Accounts
1. **Instagram to Facebook:**
   - Connect @hcjk_collection to Facebook page
   - Enable cross-posting
   - Share Instagram stories to Facebook

2. **Pinterest to Facebook:**
   - Share Pinterest boards on Facebook
   - Cross-promote content

3. **Website Integration:**
   - Add social media icons to website footer
   - Embed Instagram feed on website
   - Add Facebook reviews widget
   - Include Pinterest "Save" buttons on blog posts

### Consistent Branding
- Use same logo across all platforms
- Maintain consistent color scheme (Beige, Black, Merlot)
- Use similar bio/about text
- Cross-reference other platforms in bios

### Content Repurposing
1. **Blog Post ‚Üí Multiple Platforms:**
   - Pinterest: Create pin graphic
   - Facebook: Share with excerpt
   - Instagram: Share key points in carousel

2. **Photo Session ‚Üí Multiple Platforms:**
   - Instagram: Best 3-5 photos
   - Facebook: Album with 10-15 photos
   - Pinterest: Individual pins with descriptions
   - Website: Full gallery

3. **Client Testimonial ‚Üí Multiple Platforms:**
   - Facebook: Full review post
   - Instagram: Quote graphic
   - Pinterest: Testimonial pin
   - Website: Reviews page

## Part 5: Growth Strategies

### Pinterest Growth
1. Join group boards in your niche
2. Collaborate with wedding planners and venues
3. Create seasonal boards (holiday cards, spring portraits)
4. Use Pinterest Trends tool for keyword research
5. Enable Pinterest Shopping (if selling prints)

### Facebook Growth
1. Invite existing contacts to like page
2. Run "Like" campaigns targeting local area
3. Partner with local businesses for cross-promotion
4. Host Facebook Live Q&A sessions
5. Create Facebook Events for mini-sessions
6. Encourage clients to check in at locations
7. Join local community groups (follow rules)

### Cross-Promotion
1. Email newsletter with social links
2. Business cards with social handles
3. Include social media in contracts
4. Add social icons to email signature
5. Mention social media during consultations

## Part 8: Legal Considerations

### Client Permissions
- Always get written permission to share client photos
- Include social media usage in contracts
- Respect client privacy preferences
- Tag clients only with permission

### Copyright Protection
- Watermark images shared on social media
- Include copyright notice in image descriptions
- Monitor for unauthorized use
- Take action against copyright infringement

### Platform Policies
- Follow Pinterest Community Guidelines
- Adhere to Facebook Commerce Policies
- Respect intellectual property rights
- Avoid spam and misleading content
